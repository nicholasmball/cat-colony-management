Invite acceptance & account lifecycle

Building on the Requirements. Invite-only, minimal personal data. The crux: turning an invite into a real login when sign-ups are disabled.

The flow (recommended)

Admin fills invite (email + role)→ server: insert invite row + Supabase inviteUserByEmail→ volunteer gets email→ opens link → /accept?token=…→ sets a password→ accept_invitation(token)→ member created → into the app
Why this way: it reuses the accept_invitation RPC we already have (which requires a logged-in, email-matched account) and uses Supabase's own invite email — no new email service needed for the MVP. The created account's email always matches the invite, so the RPC's email check passes. Alternative considered: passwordless magic-link/OTP — fewer steps, but volunteers then need email access every login; a set-once password suits shared/field devices better.

States, guardrails & a11y

  • Invite states: pending (not accepted) · accepted (becomes a member) · revoked (row deleted).
  • Errors handled: expired/used token, email mismatch, already a member, email already has an account (send a sign-in link instead of a new invite).
  • Guardrails: can't deactivate the last admin; only admins see Members; all enforced by RLS, not just the UI.
  • Leaver = deactivate (soft-delete membership) — access gone immediately, nothing personal to erase (GDPR by design).
  • ≥44px targets, labelled fields, destructive actions (Revoke/Deactivate) ask for confirmation and are styled distinctly; responsive 375/768/1280.

Note: Supabase's built-in invite email is rate-limited on the free tier (fine for occasional volunteer onboarding). Production-grade email is the separate Transactional email setup task.
