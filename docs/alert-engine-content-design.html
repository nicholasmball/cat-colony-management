Alert engine & rules · content + flow design (step 2 of 6)

Notification message catalog & data-flow

This card is a backend engine with no screen — the in-app notification centre is the next card. So the design deliverables here are the things the engine and the next UI both need: the bilingual message catalog (EN + European Portuguese) every alert renders from, and a data-flow diagram of detection → record → (deferred) dispatch. Copy reuses the wording already shipped in the app — fed / pending / missed, urgent, new · unconfirmed, not seen / concern — so an alert reads in the same voice as the dashboard it points back to.

LSeverity legend

Two tiers only, matching the org's incident-urgency model. Severity is carried by an icon + word, never colour alone — the next card renders against this so a volunteer glancing at the list (or a colour-blind one) can tell urgent from routine without relying on the tint.

▲ Urgent · push + SMS ● Routine · in-app + email

The ▲ triangle reuses the app's existing urgent treatment (the red Urgent incident badge); routine uses a neutral ● dot. The label text — Urgent / Routine — is the source of truth, not the colour.

1Alert message catalog

A new alerts i18n namespace (added to messages/en.json + messages/pt.json), keyed alerts.<type>.title / .body to match the existing dotted-namespace style (concern.notSeenDays, incidents.type.poisoning). The engine stores only the message_key + message_params; copy is produced at display time via t(message_key, params). Titles are kept ≤ ~40 chars; bodies are one scannable, honest sentence. Plural-sensitive numbers use ICU {count, plural, …} exactly as the concern namespace already does.

alerts.feeding_missed ● Routine Fires when: cron finds no feeding recorded and the window closed > threshold (default 12h) ago.
Title EN Feeding missed: {colonyName}
PT Alimentação em falta: {colonyName}
Body EN No feeding recorded today and the window closed over {hours}h ago.
PT Nenhuma alimentação registada hoje e o horário fechou há mais de {hours}h.
params {colonyName}{hours}
Channel intent: in-app + email (digest-friendly — not interrupt-worthy).
alerts.incident_urgent ▲ Urgent Fires when: a feeder reports an incident at an urgency level with alerts_immediately = true.
Title EN Urgent: {incidentType} at {colonyName}
PT Urgente: {incidentType} em {colonyName}
Body EN {reporterName} reported it just now. Open the incident to triage.
PT {reporterName} reportou agora mesmo. Abra o incidente para fazer a triagem.
params {incidentType}{colonyName}{reporterName}
Channel intent: push + SMS (immediate). {incidentType} is rendered from incidents.type.* so it reads "Poisoning" / "Envenenamento", not the raw enum.
alerts.incident_routine ● Routine Fires when: an incident is reported at a non-urgent level (alerts_immediately = false).
Title EN New incident: {incidentType} at {colonyName}
PT Novo incidente: {incidentType} em {colonyName}
Body EN {reporterName} reported it. Not flagged urgent — review when you can.
PT {reporterName} reportou. Não sinalizado como urgente — reveja quando puder.
params {incidentType}{colonyName}{reporterName}
Channel intent: in-app + email.
alerts.new_cat ● Routine Fires when: a feeder reports a new cat (status new_unconfirmed) awaiting caretaker review.
Title EN New cat to confirm at {colonyName}
PT Novo gato por confirmar em {colonyName}
Body EN {reporterName} reported "{catName}". Confirm it or reject a duplicate.
PT {reporterName} reportou "{catName}". Confirme-o ou rejeite um duplicado.
params {colonyName}{catName}{reporterName}
Channel intent: in-app + email. {catName} falls back to "Unnamed cat" / "Gato sem nome" via catLabel() — never blank.
alerts.concern ● Routine Fires when: a feeder logs a sighting with status concern on a cat (the latest signal is concern).
Title EN Concern flagged: {catName}
PT Preocupação sinalizada: {catName}
Body EN {reporterName} flagged a concern at {colonyName}. Review when you can.
PT {reporterName} sinalizou uma preocupação em {colonyName}. Reveja quando puder.
params {catName}{colonyName}{reporterName}
Channel intent: in-app + email. Wording reuses the concern.flagged "Sinalizado: preocupação" voice.
alerts.not_seen ● Routine Fires when: cron's concern scan finds a cat past the not-seen window (default 7 days) or the repeated-not-seen run (default 3 in a row).
Title EN Cat not seen: {catName}
PT Gato não visto: {catName}
Body (days) EN Not seen for {count, plural, one {# day} other {# days}} at {colonyName} — needs review.
PT Não visto há {count, plural, one {# dia} other {# dias}} em {colonyName} — precisa de revisão.
Body (repeat) EN Not seen {count, plural, one {# time} other {# times}} in a row at {colonyName} — needs review.
PT Não visto {count, plural, one {# vez} other {# vezes}} seguidas em {colonyName} — precisa de revisão.
params {catName}{colonyName}{count}{reason}
Channel intent: in-app + email. {reason} (not_seen_days | repeated_not_seen) selects the body sub-key, mirroring concernReasonKey(). Copy deliberately says "needs review" — never auto-marks missing (human review only).
Engine stores key + params, not text. Every alert row holds a message_key and a JSON message_params blob — not a rendered string. The recipient's locale is applied at read time with t(message_key, params), so the same row reads English to one caretaker and Portuguese to another, and copy fixes ship by editing the catalog without a data migration.

2Data-flow: detection → record → (deferred) dispatch

Two sources feed one insert path. Real-time events insert non-blockingly off the user action; a Vercel Cron sweep handles the time-based alerts. Both pass through a dedup gate, then fan out to one row per recipient with a recorded channel intent. Actual sending is out of scope for this card — that boundary is drawn explicitly below.
