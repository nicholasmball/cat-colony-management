Street Cats of Tavira · the colony app

When a cat needs attention, how should the app reach your team?

The app now keeps an eye on the colonies for you — it notices when a feeding has been missed, when there's an emergency (an injured or poisoned cat, a dog threat), when a new cat turns up, or when a cat hasn't been seen for a while. Right now it shows all of that inside the app.

The next step is making sure the right person actually hears about it — even when they're not looking at the app. There are three ways to do that, and there are just a handful of choices that only you can make. They're all below in plain terms. 🐾

First, a simple idea to agree on Emergencies (injured/poisoned cat, dog threat) should reach people straight away. Everyday updates (a missed feed, a new cat, a cat not seen lately) can wait for a gentle once-a-day note. Does that feel right to you?

📧 Email no cost

Best for the everyday updates.

A friendly summary by email of the non-urgent things, so your caretakers can glance at it in their own time. This costs nothing.

What we'd like to know
  • Happy for the app to email these everyday updates — and to also send the "you've been invited to Street Cats" emails when you add a new volunteer?
  • Would you prefer one tidy summary once a day rather than an email every time something happens? (We'd suggest the daily summary.)

📲 A pop-up on the phone no cost

Best for emergencies — instant and free.

For emergencies, the app can make a notification pop up on a caretaker's phone the moment it happens — the quickest way to get someone's attention, at no cost. It works for any team member who has added the app to their phone and said yes to alerts (a quick, one-time setup we'll walk them through). For anyone who hasn't, the text message below is the reliable backup.

What we'd like to know Happy for these phone pop-ups to be used for emergencies? (We'd keep them for emergencies only, so phones don't buzz over everyday things.)

💬 A text message small cost

The dependable backup for emergencies — it always gets through.

A text message is the one thing that reaches a phone with no app and no internet needed — the reliable way to make sure an emergency isn't missed. It's also the only option with a small cost and one thing for you to weigh up.

The cost is tiny — each text is around 8 cents, and only for genuine emergencies. In a typical month that's likely just a euro or two; even a busy month is only a few euros.

One thing for you to decide To send someone a text, the app has to keep their mobile number. That's a small step away from your wish to hold as little about volunteers as possible — so it's your call. We'd suggest only keeping numbers for the few people who'd handle emergencies, and only with their say-so.
What we'd like to know
  • Are you comfortable keeping mobile numbers for emergency texts (just for the people who'd respond to them)?
  • Who should get an emergency text — everyone who manages colonies, or a small "on-call" group you choose?
  • Roughly how many emergencies do you get in a month, and how many people would each one text? (So we can give you an exact cost.)

🎛️ Letting people choose

Should each caretaker be able to choose how they're reached — for example "email yes, texts no"? We'd suggest yes, so everyone gets it the way that suits them.
